The task of this project was to create a website which explained how wireless signals worked and to introduce a technology called FSS (Frequency Selective Surfaces).

The main feature is a HTML5 canvas element which interactively shows how wireless access points behave in a variety of situations by showing green (good) and red (bad) signal areas. The applet has 6 scenarios; each one introducing more wireless access points, walls, changing the access points' channel/transmit power and also the ability to change the walls' resistance.

The content of this site was produced by Dr John Batchelor (Reader in Antenna Technology at the University of Kent) and Dr Michael Neve (senior lecturer in telecommunications and electromagnetics group at the University of Auckland, New Zealand).
