This project involved creating a website that explains how wireless signals work and introduces FSS (Frequency Selective Surfaces)â€”a technology that helps manage signal interference in wireless communication.

The main feature is an HTML5 canvas that shows how wireless access points behave in different situations. The interactive applet visualises the signal quality with green (good) and red (bad) areas. There are 6 scenarios, each introducing new elements like additional access points, walls, and changes to the access points' channel/transmit power. Users can also adjust the resistance of the walls.

This research was conducted by Dr. John Batchelor, Reader in Antenna Technology at the University of Kent, and Dr. Michael Neve, Senior Lecturer in Telecommunications and Electromagnetics at the University of Auckland, New Zealand.
